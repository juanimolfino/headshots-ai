## 1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. Similar technologies (such as local storage) store information in your browser. They help websites function, remember your preferences, and keep you signed in.

## 2. How We Use Cookies and Similar Technologies

We use only what is needed to run the Service. We do **not** use cookies to sell your data or to serve third-party advertising.

| Type | Purpose | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Strictly necessary** | Keep you signed in, secure your session, and operate core features. The Service cannot work properly without these. | Authentication/session cookies; security tokens |
| **Functional / local storage** | Remember in-app preferences and interface state (for example, which dismissible notices you've hidden). | Browser local storage used by the dashboard |
| **Payment provider** | Set by Stripe during checkout to process payments securely and prevent fraud. | Stripe cookies on the checkout flow |

> If you later add analytics or marketing cookies, this section must be updated and, for users in the EEA/UK, a consent banner with opt-in for non-essential cookies will be required.

## 3. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies are set by third parties we rely on, most notably **Stripe** for secure payment processing. These providers process cookie data under their own privacy policies. We encourage you to review Stripe's privacy and cookie notices.

## 4. Managing Cookies

Most browsers let you view, block, or delete cookies through their settings. Blocking **strictly necessary** cookies may prevent you from signing in or using core features of the Service. You can also clear your browser's local storage, which will reset in-app interface preferences.
